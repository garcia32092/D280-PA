Tucson, Arizona

  • Population: Tucson had a population of approximately 542,629 people as of the 2020 United States Census, making it the second-largest city in Arizona after Phoenix.
  • Year Incorporated: Tucson was incorporated as a city in 1877.
  • Region: Tucson is located in the southeastern part of Arizona, in the Sonoran Desert. It is about 110 miles southeast of Phoenix and is part of Pima County.
  • Classification: Urban.
  • Average Income: The median household income in Tucson was estimated to be around $43,425, according to data from the U.S. Census Bureau's American Community Survey for 2019. This is lower than the median household income for the entire state of Arizona, which was approximately $62,055 at the same period.
